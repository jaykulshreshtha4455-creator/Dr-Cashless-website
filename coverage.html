Watch Out

Common Exclusions

Things most health insurance policies do not cover — know before you claim.

Waiting Period

Pre-existing diseases are typically not covered for the first 2–4 years. Specific illnesses like cataract, hernia, joint replacement have 1–2 year waiting periods.

Dental Treatment

Routine dental procedures, fillings, root canals, braces, and dental implants are excluded in most standard health policies unless specifically added as a rider.

Eyesight & Specs

Correction of eyesight, spectacles, contact lenses, and LASIK surgery for refractive errors are generally excluded unless covered by a specific rider.

Maternity (Initial)

Maternity benefits usually activate after 9–24 months. Complications due to pregnancy in the first policy year are often excluded.

Cosmetic Surgery

Plastic surgery, aesthetic procedures, liposuction, hair transplants, and any treatment that is not medically necessary are excluded.

Self-inflicted / Addiction

Injuries due to self-harm, drug abuse, alcoholism, or any treatment arising from intoxication or substance abuse is not covered by any insurer.

Weight & Wellness

Obesity treatment, weight-loss surgery, gym injuries unless admitted for 24+ hours, and general wellness procedures are excluded in most policies.

Adventure Sports Injuries

Injuries sustained while doing adventure sports (skydiving, bungee jumping, racing) are typically excluded unless a specific rider is purchased.
